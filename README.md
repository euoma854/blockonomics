**How the merchant billing actually works:**

You don't pay per month. You pay 1% of whatever Bitcoin revenue you receive through the platform. Blockonomics sends a consolidated monthly bill on the 1st of the following month. You top up a "credit balance" in BTC to cover it. If your balance runs low, your account stays active for the rest of the month — you're not suddenly cut off mid-transaction.

Example: you receive 0.25 BTC in a month → fee is 0.0025 BTC. Simple.

**What about promo codes?**

Blockonomics doesn't run traditional coupon campaigns. The "deal" is the model itself: free to start, 1% after that, no monthly minimums, no setup charges, no surprise fees. For context, competing custodial gateways often take 2-5% when you factor in price manipulation and withdrawal fees. At 1%, Blockonomics is genuinely one of the lower-cost options in the space.

## One Thing Worth Knowing

Blockonomics cannot distinguish between personal and merchant payments going to the same wallet. If you link a wallet to Blockonomics, use it exclusively for merchant transactions — keep personal Bitcoin payments going to a separate wallet. This is called out clearly in their docs, and if you accidentally get charged for a personal payment, they'll refund it if you raise a ticket.
